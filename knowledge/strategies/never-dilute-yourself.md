# Never Dilute Yourself

Source: Never Dilute Yourself by Alex Hormozi
URL: https://youtube.com/watch?v=3xTcFmn8yQ0
Extracted: 2026-04-20
Businesses: all

## What it is
Dilution happens when you spread identity, energy, or positioning across too many targets at once. Hormozi argues that the people who win long-term refuse to water down their message, their offer, or their standards -- even when faced with pressure to appeal to more people or enter more markets.

## How it works
- Identify your one core position (what you are known for and who you serve).
- When opportunities outside that core appear, apply a filter: does this sharpen or dilute what you stand for?
- If it dilutes, decline -- even when the short-term revenue looks attractive.
- Reinvest attention into deepening the core instead of widening the perimeter.

## BridgeWorks application
Emmanuel runs three distinct brands: BridgeWorks (agency), his personal brand, and MindOfAKing (faith/poetry). The risk of dilution is real. Each brand should have a clear positioning statement and audience, and content or offers should not cross-pollinate unless there is a deliberate strategic reason. For BridgeWorks specifically, staying focused on SMEs in Nigeria and CEE rather than chasing global generalist agency work keeps positioning sharp and referrals clean.
