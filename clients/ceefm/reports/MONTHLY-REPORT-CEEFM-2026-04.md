### PageSpeed Performance Audit (Pre-Patch Baseline, 2026-04-30)

| Surface | Performance | FCP | LCP | TBT | CLS |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Desktop | 94 / 100 | 0.9s | 1.2s | Good | 0.01 |
| Mobile | 76 / 100 | 2.7s | 5.0s | 100ms | 0.01 |

Desktop landed cleanly in the green band on the first measurement. Mobile flagged Largest Contentful Paint at 5.0s, which Google's threshold treats as Poor (target under 2.5s). Root cause: the font cascade was loading 24+ woff2 files including unused Cyrillic and Vietnamese subsets, blocking the critical render path.

A performance patch was applied in source on 2026-04-30:

- Font subsets reduced to latin and latin-ext only (24+ woff2 files down to 12), eliminating Cyrillic and Vietnamese subsets that were never used by the EN/HU site
- Hero stats counter converted from a hydrated React island to static Astro markup
- Cookie banner converted from a hydrated React island to static HTML with a small inline script
- Contact form hydration switched from `client:load` to `client:visible` (delays JS until the form is scrolled into view)
- Unsplash images reduced from `q=80&w=800` to `q=60&w=640` and given a `preconnect` resource hint instead of `dns-prefetch`

Deployment verification and re-test against the patch are queued. Expected mobile lift: Performance from 76 toward 85+ and LCP from 5.0s toward 2.5s. Desktop already in the Good band; expecting incremental improvement only.

Accessibility currently sits at 88 mobile and 84 desktop. Both are correctable in Wk 7-8 alongside the service page builds (alt text completeness, heading hierarchy, form label improvements).

## 6. Strategic Findings from the Marketing Audit

The full marketing audit (`MARKETING-AUDIT-2026-04-30.md`) ran today as the strategic complement to the GEO audit. Five findings worth surfacing here.

**Finding 1: Full-funnel conversion estimate.** Visit-to-signed-contract today benchmarks at 0.13 to 0.45%. At plausible 1,000 monthly visits this produces 1 to 4 signed contracts per month. Tightening the funnel without additional ad spend can plausibly double this number inside a 90-day window.

**Finding 2: The form is buried.** It sits at section 9 of 9 on a single-page scroll. No mid-page CTA appears between Limehome (section 6) and the form. Every scroll between those two sections is a leak point. A mid-page CTA card immediately after the Limehome metrics is the single highest-lift intervention available, ahead of any messaging change.

**Finding 3: Single-reference risk.** Limehome is the only named reference on the entire site. The Limehome quote is unattributed (no name, no role). For a Hungarian property manager evaluating an FM switch, "one logo" reads as "one client". Adding a second case study or even a logo bar with anonymised references ("a 350-unit residential complex, District XIII") materially shifts trust.

**Finding 4: Funnel split.** The current homepage tries to convert a Limehome-style boutique-hospitality buyer and a residential-condo közös képviselő with the same hero, the same case study, and the same form. These are two different sales motions. Splitting the homepage into hospitality and residential landing pages with separate proof points is the single biggest 90-day intervention available. Recommended for May Wk 7-8 build.

**Finding 5: Aparthotel Operating Standard play.** None of the named Hungarian competitors (B+N, ATALIAN, Prizma, ISS HU, Sodexo HU, Strabag PFS, KÉSZ) have built a hospitality-first content layer or a named aparthotel reference. Publishing a "Aparthotel Operating Standard v1": a 14-point operating framework derived from the Limehome work: before they notice the niche is unguarded raises the cost of imitation and gives sales a document to send instead of a deck. Recommended for May Wk 7-8 draft, June ship.

## 7. The Pricing Anchor Question (For the May Call)

The market audit recommends CEEFM publish a public **floor price** anchor: not full pricing, just floor. Pattern:

> "Premium FM contracts start from EUR 2,800/month for residential blocks, EUR 4,500/month for aparthotels, EUR 9,000/month for boutique hotels. Final pricing depends on property size, frequency, and service mix. Request a site assessment for a quote."

**Upside.** Filters price tourists below the floor. Saves the team time on disqualified inquiries. Signals premium without revealing deal economics. Gives ad copy a qualifier line. Breaks ranks with the entire competitive set, all of whom hide pricing.

**Downside.** Commits CEEFM to a public floor. Some buyers anchor to the floor as their negotiation start. If competitors imitate, the differentiator disappears.

**My read.** This is a strategic move worth making, because the qualified-lead lift (estimated +20 to 35% on form-to-walkthrough rate) compounds across every subsequent month of the engagement. But it commits CEEFM commercially, so it needs your explicit yes before it ships.

**Asked of Victor at the May call:** approve floor anchors yes/no. If yes, the EUR thresholds above need confirmation against your actual cost economics.
