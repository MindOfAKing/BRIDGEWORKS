## 1. Sequence Architecture

| Email | Purpose | Send timing | Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Introduction + credibility anchor (Limehome) | Day 0 | 90-110 words |
| 2 | Value proposition + case study link | Day 4 (business days) | 100-120 words |
| 3 | Direct ask + soft close | Day 9 (business days from Email 1) | 70-90 words |

If a prospect replies to any email, the sequence pauses automatically. If a prospect clicks through to `/contact` or `/hu/kapcsolat` and submits the form, the sequence pauses and the form submission triggers the standard inbound flow.

## 5. Sending Platform and Operational Notes

### Recommended platform

**Apollo.io** or **Smartlead** for the sending infrastructure. Both:
- Connect via SMTP to `office@ceefm.eu`
- Send from a domain-warmed inbox to avoid spam filtering
- Track opens, clicks, replies
- Pause sequences automatically on reply
- A/B test subject lines

Cost: roughly EUR 30-50/month for 500-1000 sends/month, well within the EUR 80/month cold outreach retainer line.

### Pre-flight checklist before launch

| Item | Status | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| `office@ceefm.eu` SPF, DKIM, DMARC verified | To verify | BridgeWorks |
| Mailbox warm-up (2-week ramp from 10 sends/day to 50/day) | Not started | BridgeWorks |
| Target list cleaned (deliverability check, deduplication) | Not started | BridgeWorks |
| Personalisation tokens populated ([FirstName], [Company], [PropertyType]) | N/A until list ready | BridgeWorks |
| Sequence loaded into platform with reply detection | Not started | BridgeWorks |
| GEO score above 70 (so prospects see a credible site when they click through) | 61/100 today | Joint |

### Target list size

Proposal commits to 50 prospects in Phase 2 (Wk 3-4) and expansion to 150 in Phase 3 (Wk 5-8). Per the artifact's own analysis, the realistic Budapest-area pool is closer to 50-100 high-fit prospects. Recommended: build a 100-prospect list in two waves of 50.

### Outcome targets (KPIs)

- Open rate: above 40% on Email 1 (cold-warmed, EU sender)
- Reply rate: above 6% across the full 3-email sequence
- Click-to-`/contact`: above 2% of opens
- Site-assessment requests from outreach: above 1% of total prospects (= 1 form fill per 100 prospects, conservative)
- Signed contracts attributable: 1-2 per 100 prospects in the first cohort, more once social proof builds

### What NOT to do

- Do not buy a list. Build manually from LinkedIn Sales Navigator filters and Cégadatbázis (Hungarian company registry).
- Do not include attachments. Adds spam-filter risk and most prospects do not open PDFs from cold email.
- Do not use sender names other than "Victor Danmagaji" or "Victor at CEEFM", sender consistency builds reply rate.
- Do not personalise the body beyond `[FirstName]` and `[Company]` and one industry token. Heavy personalisation is hard to scale and does not lift reply rate above well-written generic copy at this volume.

## 6. Translation Discipline

The HU and EN versions are intentionally not literal translations of each other. Hungarian buyers read different sentence rhythms and respond to slightly different framings (less direct ask, more relationship signalling). Both versions have been drafted natively in their own register.

**Do not run the EN version through DeepL to produce the HU version.** The HU draft above is hand-written for the audience.

## 7. Prospect Segmentation

The same 3-email sequence can run with light token swaps for three different prospect segments. Use the right `[PropertyType]` token per segment:

| Segment | `[PropertyType]` token | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Aparthotel operators | "aparthotel" / "apartmanhotel" | "translates directly to other aparthotel operators" |
| Property management companies | "residential portfolio" / "lakossági portfólió" | "translates directly to other residential portfolios" |
| Boutique hotel GMs | "boutique hotel" / "butikhotel" | "translates directly to other boutique hotel operators" |

Three separate sequences in the platform, same body, different token. Higher reply rate than a one-size-fits-all version.
