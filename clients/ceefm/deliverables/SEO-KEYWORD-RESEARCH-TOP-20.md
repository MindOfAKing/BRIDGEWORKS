## How this list was built

Keywords were selected against three filters:

1. **Buyer-intent fit:** does someone with money to spend on facility management actually type this phrase into Google?
2. **CEEFM ranking realism:** can a 2-page site (today) realistically rank in the top 10 within 90 days, or is the term dominated by directories and large players?
3. **Conversion potential:** how directly does the search lead to a site assessment request, not just a content page view?

Search volumes are stated as Low / Medium / High because Google Keyword Planner data is gated. The qualitative tier is enough for prioritisation; absolute volume figures will be verified once the GBP and Search Console are connected.

Hungarian keywords carry more weight than English because the buyer (property manager, hotel GM, condo board) is overwhelmingly Hungarian-language. Of the top 20, 13 are HU-primary, 5 are EN-primary (Budapest expat operators, multinational hotel chains), and 2 are bilingual brand-defence terms.

## Per-keyword commentary (deeper notes for the top 5)

### #1: `takarítás Budapest aparthotel` (HU)

This is the single highest-leverage keyword for CEEFM. The Limehome partnership directly demonstrates expertise. Almost no Hungarian cleaning contractor competes here because most cleaning firms target generic offices. CEEFM should own this term.

**Why it converts:** the searcher is a Hungarian aparthotel operator looking for a vetted cleaning service for short-stay properties. This is exactly Limehome's profile. A landing page that leads with "Limehome 9.4 cleanliness score, 24 months above 9.0" plus a site-assessment CTA should convert at well above industry baseline.

**On-page targets:** title tag with the exact phrase, H1 within 4 words of it, the Limehome metric block above the fold, FAQ block addressing pricing and contract terms, ContactPage schema link.

### #2: `társasház takarítás Budapest` (HU)

Massive ongoing demand. Every Hungarian condo (társasház) needs cleaning, and the buyer is the elected közös képviselő (building manager). The competitor field is fragmented (small local cleaners) so the page does not need to outrank any large brand, just produce a more credible-looking offer.

**Why it converts:** building managers compare 3 to 5 quotes when retendering. A page that opens with "98% client retention, EU-compliant documentation, dedicated account manager" reads as a different category than the typical low-cost cleaner.

**On-page targets:** include legal language about NAV-compliant invoicing, GDPR-safe service logs, and a downloadable sample monthly report. These are common buyer asks that filter cleaners.

### #3: `facility management Budapest` (EN)

Expat property managers, multinational hotel chains, and CEE-region heads of real estate search this phrase. The competition (B+N, ATALIAN, Prizma) ranks high but the artifact's own analysis confirms none of them target the boutique aparthotel niche.

**Why it converts:** the English query filters for a buyer who values international standards: exactly the segment CEEFM is best positioned for.

**On-page targets:** lead with the Limehome metric, follow with EU compliance, then the integrated FM proposition (cleaning + maintenance + waste + hygiene under one contract). Include English testimonial pull-quote when the third client signs.

### #4: `létesítménygazdálkodás Budapest` (HU)

This is the formal Hungarian term for facility management. Search volume is lower than #1 or #2 but buyer intent is very high. Anyone typing this phrase is procurement-savvy. Competition is surprisingly thin because most Hungarian competitors do not optimise for the formal term, preferring "takarítás" instead.

**Why it converts:** the searcher already knows what FM is and is looking for a provider. Lower search volume, much higher conversion rate per visitor.

**On-page targets:** include the formal definition in the H1, technical description of the contract model, KPI list, EU-compliance documentation framework.

### #5: `szálloda takarítás Budapest` (HU)

Hotel housekeeping outsourcing. Hungary's hotel sector grew 17% YoY in early 2025. Boutique hotels and 3-4 star independents that cannot afford in-house housekeeping departments are the buyer. The Limehome reference works here too, the model translates directly.

**Why it converts:** General Managers and Operations Directors are the searchers. They are KPI-fluent and respond to numbers, not adjectives.

**On-page targets:** RevPAR sensitivity calculator (hotel rooms not turned around fast = lost room nights), guest score correlation (cleaner room = higher Booking score = +€X RevPAR), 30-second response time SLAs.

## What this list deliberately leaves out

- **`takarítás Budapest`** alone: too generic, dominated by directories, low buyer intent.
- **`olcsó takarítás Budapest`** ("cheap cleaning Budapest"): wrong buyer profile. CEEFM is premium contract-based; chasing the price-sensitive segment dilutes positioning.
- **`takarítónő`** ("cleaning lady"): residential B2C, not in scope.
- **City-secondary terms** (`takarítás Debrecen`, `takarítás Győr`): these belong to a regional expansion phase, not Phase 1 foundation.

## Implementation notes

### URL structure recommendation

Adopt this pattern across both locales:
- `/services/[service-slug]/` (EN)
- `/hu/szolgaltatasok/[service-slug]/` (HU)
- `/industries/[industry-slug]/` (EN)
- `/hu/iparagak/[industry-slug]/` (HU)
- `/case-studies/[client-slug]/` (EN)
- `/hu/esettanulmanyok/[client-slug]/` (HU)

Hungarian slugs use the localised industry term, not English transliteration. This is a stronger HU SEO signal than `/hu/services/` would be.

### Internal linking

Each new service page should link back to:
- The homepage `/` or `/hu/`
- The contact page `/contact` or `/hu/kapcsolat` (already shipping)
- Two related service pages (cross-linking improves PageRank flow)
- The Limehome case study page once it exists

### Schema additions per page

For each Tier 1 service page, add `Service` schema with:
- `name` (the service)
- `provider` referencing the existing `#organization` node
- `areaServed` (Hungary, with geo radius)
- `description`
- `offers.priceSpecification` (range, e.g. "from 200 EUR/month per property")

### Content depth target

Each Tier 1 page: minimum 800 words of original copy. Hungarian-language pages: write directly in Hungarian, do not machine-translate from English. Local idiom matters for trust.

### Tracking

Once Search Console is connected and pages are indexed:
- Track each keyword's average position (target: top 10 within 90 days for Tier 1, top 20 for Tier 2)
- Track impressions per keyword
- Track CTR (target above 3% for ranked positions)
- Track click-to-form-submission rate per page (target above 1.5% for service pages, above 4% for the contact page)
